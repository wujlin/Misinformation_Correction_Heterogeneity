# Misinformation Correction and Network Heterogeneity

## Working Title

Epistemic and Expressive Mechanisms of Public Correction in Heterogeneous Networks

## Core Research Question

This project examines how network heterogeneity shapes information checking behavior in online discussions. The central question is:

> How does heterogeneity affect users' ability to recognize misinformation and their willingness to publicly correct it?

The current idea is not to study correction as a simple individual behavior. The project treats correction as a networked behavior shaped by information exposure, audience structure, and social accountability.

## Motivation

Existing research often suggests that heterogeneous networks are beneficial for information exposure, cross-cutting discussion, and misinformation recognition. Users connected to different communities may encounter more diverse information cues, alternative interpretations, and corrective information.

Recent studies on cross-cutting exposure, context collapse, and online self-censorship suggest another possibility. Heterogeneous audiences may also increase anticipated social accountability, conflict risk, and relationship costs. As a result, heterogeneity may improve users' ability to recognize misinformation while weakening their willingness to correct it publicly.

The project therefore focuses on the gap between private recognition and public correction. This gap matters because misinformation can remain publicly visible even when some users privately recognize that the information is problematic.

## Proposed Model

The project proposes an epistemic-expressive model of public correction.

The model distinguishes two mechanisms:

1. Epistemic mechanism:
   Network structural heterogeneity may increase exposure to diverse informational cues and improve misinformation recognition.

2. Expressive mechanism:
   Audience attribute heterogeneity may increase anticipated social accountability and suppress public correction.

The key point is not that heterogeneity is simply good or bad. The key point is that heterogeneity may operate through different mechanisms at the same time.

## Current Contribution Claim

The expected contribution is to explain why correction capacity does not automatically become correction behavior. Users who are structurally positioned to recognize misinformation may also face mixed audiences and higher social accountability, which can inhibit public correction.

The project can contribute to misinformation correction, cross-cutting exposure, online self-censorship, and computational communication research.

## Methodological Plan

The project follows a computational communication approach.

Network analysis will be used to measure network structural heterogeneity, audience attribute heterogeneity, brokerage position, and ego-network diversity.

Agent-based modeling will be used to simulate how individual recognition and correction decisions accumulate into group-level misinformation persistence.

LLM agents may be used to construct behaviorally heterogeneous agents with different information exposure, confidence, correction thresholds, and expression concerns. LLM agents should support the simulation design rather than become the theoretical core.

## Current Status

The current direction is more promising than previous ideas around AI-generated fake news, perceived majority, and public comment diversity because it has a clearer social psychological mechanism and stronger connection to network structure.

The next step is to refine the mechanism, define measurable variables, identify empirical contexts, and decide whether the study should use survey experiment, observational network data, simulation, or a mixed design.

## Updated Reddit Direction

After feasibility discussion, the current empirical direction is Reddit-based correction misallocation. The revised question is whether public corrections occur where misinformation correction is most needed, or whether correction supply is concentrated in communities that are already skeptical of the misinformation.
