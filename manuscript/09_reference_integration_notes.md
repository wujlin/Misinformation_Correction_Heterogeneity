# Reference Integration Notes

## Purpose

This note records how the manuscript currently connects its contributions to prior work. It is a writing map separate from the final bibliography.

The working BibTeX file is `references.bib`.

## Correction Effectiveness and Misinformation Persistence

Use these references to position the supply-side contribution:

- Lewandowsky et al. (2012): misinformation correction, continued influence, and debiasing.
- Chan et al. (2017): meta-analysis of debunking messages.
- Walter and Murphy (2018): meta-analysis of misinformation correction.
- Ecker et al. (2022): psychological drivers of misinformation belief and resistance to correction.

Manuscript role:

- These studies support the claim that much correction research asks what happens after correction exposure.
- The present study adds the prior behavioral condition: whether correction appears in the local discussion.

## Social-Media Correction

Use these references to connect the study to platform-based correction:

- Bode and Vraga (2015): correction of misinformation through related stories functionality in social media.
- Vraga and Bode (2017): expert-source correction of health misinformation in social media.

Manuscript role:

- These studies support the claim that corrective responses can reduce misperceptions after users encounter them.
- The present study shifts the outcome from correction effect to public correction supply.

## Network Heterogeneity and Expression

Use these references to support the capacity-activation distinction:

- Mutz (2002, 2006): cross-cutting exposure, heterogeneous networks, and participation trade-offs.
- Noelle-Neumann (1974): spiral of silence and public expression under perceived social pressure.
- Marwick and boyd (2011): context collapse and imagined audiences.
- Hampton et al. (2014): social media and spiral of silence.

Manuscript role:

- Mutz supports the correction-capacity side through heterogeneous exposure.
- Noelle-Neumann, Marwick and boyd, and Hampton et al. support the correction-activation side through visibility, audience response, and self-censorship.

## Agent-Based Modeling

Use these references to position the ABM:

- Epstein (1999): agent-based computational models and generative social science.
- Epstein (2006): generative social science and the logic of growing macro-level patterns from micro-level rules.

Manuscript role:

- The ABM should be described as generative evidence and scenario exploration.
- The ABM should be framed as calibrated scenario evidence for observable correction activation.
