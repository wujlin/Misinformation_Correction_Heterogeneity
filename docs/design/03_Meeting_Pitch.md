# Meeting Pitch

## Two-Paragraph Version

Existing research often treats heterogeneity as beneficial for information exposure and misinformation recognition because users can encounter different communities, viewpoints, and corrective cues. However, recent research on cross-cutting exposure, context collapse, and online self-censorship suggests a more complex possibility. Heterogeneity may not directly translate into public correction. Users who face mixed audiences may anticipate higher social accountability, conflict risk, and relationship costs. Therefore, the effect of heterogeneity on information checking behavior needs to be unpacked. The key question is how heterogeneity shapes both users' ability to recognize misinformation and their willingness to correct it publicly.

This project proposes an epistemic-expressive model of public correction. The model distinguishes two mechanisms. The epistemic mechanism focuses on how network structural heterogeneity affects information exposure and misinformation recognition. The expressive mechanism focuses on how audience attribute heterogeneity affects anticipated social accountability and public correction inhibition. Methodologically, the project follows a computational communication approach. Network analysis will be used to measure structural heterogeneity, audience heterogeneity, and brokerage positions. Agent-based modeling will be used to simulate how individual-level recognition and correction decisions accumulate into group-level misinformation persistence. LLM agents may be used to construct heterogeneous agents with different information exposure, confidence, correction thresholds, and expression concerns, but the theoretical core remains the epistemic-expressive mechanism.

## One-Sentence Version

This project examines why heterogeneous networks may improve misinformation recognition while inhibiting public correction, focusing on the epistemic and expressive mechanisms that create a gap between private recognition and public correction.

## Short Chinese Version

这个研究关注异质性网络如何影响 misinformation correction。既有研究通常认为异质性有利于信息接触、观点交叉和错误信息识别，但 cross-cutting exposure、context collapse 和 online self-censorship 的研究也说明，异质性可能带来更高的社会问责和表达成本。因此，本文不把 heterogeneity 视为单一变量，而是提出一个 epistemic-expressive model。结构异质性可能通过增加多元信息线索提高 private recognition；受众属性异质性可能通过提高 social accountability 抑制 public correction。方法上，本文结合 network analysis、agent-based modeling 和 LLM agents，模拟个体层面的识别和纠错决策如何在不同网络生成机制下累积为群体层面的 misinformation persistence。

## Questions for Advisor

- Should the paper position itself primarily in misinformation correction, cross-cutting exposure, or computational communication?
- Which empirical context is more feasible: social media discussion threads, survey experiment, or synthetic ABM calibrated by human data?
- Should LLM agents be a central method, or should they only be used after a survey experiment provides behavioral parameters?
- Which outcome is stronger: public correction intention, actual correction behavior, or misinformation persistence in simulated networks?
